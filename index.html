Brand Focus

With a rich background in marketing and design, I hold strongly to established style guidelines when creating a user experience. I understand the importance of a consistent brand message, and believe the design of an interface must match all other user touchpoints in order to maintain a positive reflection of a brand.

Responsive Design

Depending on what we build together, you may find your largest userbase interacts with your brand and tools primarily through portable devices like phones and tablets. That's why there is a strong emphasis on mobile-friendly web interface design and responsiveness. I make it a regular practice to thoroughly test my creations on various platforms, checking performance, load times, usability, readability, and flow. I keep up-to-date on the latest frameworks and tools that assist in optimizing the user interface in these areas.

Story Boarding

Crafting a user experience can be a time-consuming process. The faster we can conceptualize a user's interactions with the tool, the quicker we can start building it out. Creating a user story is a fantastic way I can quickly portray a concept to a client. This can be done through a story board presentation that shows the functionality of each button and interactive element. It can be done through an interactive PDF or through UI/UX design tools such as Adobe XD or Figma. The benefit to storyboarding is that we can quickly share a target vision with the larger team, allowing departments to review the content and resolve any potential issues now rather than when they surface late in development.

Feedback Loops

YIPPEE, your product just went live! That's great. But now it's time to push your user experience to the next level. A powerful practice is to set up a client feedback loop to better understand how you can improve your products and services. Methods for doing this include:

  • customer reviews
  • questionnaires
  • discussion groups
  • user analytics
By carefully reviewing what the userbase is saying, we can enhance the service offerings and tighten up weak portions of our code.

Turning a simple necessity into a powerful marketing tool for the entire team!

2014 – 2020

The idea of the "Email Signature Builder Tool" was far off when I started assisting in the creation of our branded signature designs. Initial layouts were crafted by our marketing team and forwarded on to development to write the html code. Once written, it was our job to instruct team members on how to update the code and apply their custom signature to their email client like Gmail or Outlook. Most users understood how to copy and paste the materials with ease, so this worked for a while. But the job became more complex when operations requested to have corporate headshots included for the majority of our users. Emailing over a blank signature and expecting sales members to add all the components, especially an image, was out of the question. This would be too difficult for team members to accomplish on their own and prone to brand inconsistencies. At this time, I began to take a new approach to the request.

With some basic understanding of Javascript, I knew it was possible to change the contents of various elements on a click action. This gave me the idea of using a form to dynamically update the contents of the signature design. With lots of research and comprehensive testing, I was able to put together a demo for our team that met the needs and included each user's headshot! We quickly launched the tool company-wide to ensure all users could be on-brand with their email messaging.

We pushed the "Email Signature Builder Tool" a little farther by also including customizable branding options and marketing banners for different use cases. Also, a text-only version was included for some systems that don't accept imagery. In addition, all variables can be "saved into the URL string by clicking "Save" or the "I" beam. This makes it possible for all users to have their own unique url to come back to should they need to make an update to their signature in the future.

When you check out the tool, try using my Dexter Solutions email address, "nburgher@dextersolutions.net". This will make my headshot appear!

Skills Used:
  • HTML
  • CSS
  • Javascript
  • URL Query strings
